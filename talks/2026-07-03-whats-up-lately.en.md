## on the other little projects

Notiee really is royalty! i have zero time for anything else now, it's kind of a disaster — and on top of that, final exams (as busy as i already am, i somehow started porting Notiee to android). speaking of finals though, i whipped up a little quiz-practice site called [idbettershua!](https://shua.idbetter.run) — i grind first. honestly it's just a very ordinary quiz site, nothing much to say, and it can only drill the subjects i'm personally reviewing for finals (but this one got the fastest response by far — hahaha, the whole class and even other classes in the major came to support it).

the second most-tended project is Dreamland. i really want to make it good, but it always feels off — the way it's presented, the quality of the text. carrying that feeling, i've already torn Dreamland down and rebuilt it once.

Dreamland actually started from a little quirk of mine. i love building out a whole set of settings — what kind of society this is, what kind of people, what rules, what stories happen to them. before, i could only ideate through a chatbot: i'd feed it a long string of settings first, then within my story world different characters and stories would grow, and i'd give events through one of the people or from a god's-eye view, watching things play out, deducing and discovering. i love that feeling — i'm even a little addicted (during gemini's smartest stretch i used it to play this, and a single session could run around 10 hours…). but these chatbots always have flaws: the context, and toward the later stages they often drift off-meaning, or start making things up, and it feels like the world i painstakingly built starts to collapse, the protagonists i made up start to vanish, and i gradually can't keep going.

Dreamland exists to solve exactly this pain point, and then to do focused optimization on top of it. progress isn't where i'd like it to be, but it really is a lot better than at the very start: characters now have memory, spatial awareness, money awareness, plus numeric systems for money and affinity. i hope it keeps getting better, and i hope to put out a first playable version by the end of this year.
