this one piled up for a while — i kept building bits and pieces, and i'm writing it all out today. here are the parts worth talking about, heavy ones first.

## you can write notes now — no photo needed

notiee always made you take a photo first. but a lot of the time there's just one line in your head. now, tap the plus in the top-right of the capture page to open a text note (beta) and just write it down — no photo, and it doesn't touch any of your AI quota. once you're done it's just saved, clean and simple.

## open a note, and it digs up the related ones

turn on "deep association" first — on the Today page, tap "me" in the top-right → lab features → deep association. after that, open any record and scroll to the bottom and you'll see "related notes," older entries notiee thinks are connected to this one. tap to jump straight over. all of it runs on your phone, nothing goes online, so there's zero privacy worry. it only surfaces ones that are genuinely close, three at most, no padding.

and if you jot another note under the same event within 48 hours, it'll flag "continuation" at the top, so you can trace back along the timeline.

## Spark got a big upgrade

Spark is the AI assistant inside notiee — it's the fourth tab in the bottom bar — and it got noticeably smarter this round:

- **it actually gets what you mean**: searching your records in Spark used to be keyword-only. now it does semantic search and understands what you're after. it runs on-device by default, nothing leaves your phone — and in the lab you can switch on "high-quality cloud search," using your own AI provider for sharper results.
- **the Agent can do more for you**: you can now have Spark edit the time or title of an existing event, check what weekday a date is or the gap between dates, even drop a link in chat and let it read the page's text (with built-in guardrails — it won't touch internal addresses).
- **pick your model and thinking depth**: the chat screen now has a model picker and a thinking-strength toggle. that preference is saved on its own and doesn't affect your global settings. the preset model list is refreshed to the latest too.
- **smoother conversations**: hit stop mid-generation whenever you want; search past conversations by title or message content; and the send button and title placement got tidied up.
- **clearer privacy line**: Spark won't refuse to return your own records on privacy grounds anymore — but reading anything online still needs your okay first.

## you have an identity now

new local account: set a nickname, pick an avatar, that's it. everything stays on your phone and nothing gets uploaded. once you sign in, the "me" page shows your avatar.

## color tags for your events

events on the timeline can carry tags now. four come built in — personal (blue), work (orange), class (green), temporary (purple) — and you can make your own, any name, any color. when you shoot a photo, the top of the viewfinder shows which event you're under and its color. importing a class schedule or a meeting file? you can pick a tag per entry too.

## todos: a full management page

there's a new "all todos" page (reach it from the Today page by tapping "me" in the top-right), bucketed by overdue / today / tomorrow / this week / later / no due date, with finished ones folded away. the Today page only shows what's on deck right now (five at most); tapping the todo count on Today gets you to the full list too. all of it lives on your device, no cloud needed.

## records now come in three kinds

records carry a source marker now, so you can tell them apart at a glance from the thumbnail: photo records show the photo, Spark-generated ones show a sparkles icon, and plain text notes show a document icon. the records Spark creates itself also land in a dedicated "Spark generated" folder.

## legal docs in more languages

the user agreement and privacy policy got rewritten — simplified chinese, traditional (hong kong/macau and taiwan kept separate), and english, picked automatically by your app language and region. they moved from PDF to web pages too, so they follow your system dark mode and font size. much nicer to read.

## fitted for iOS 26 liquid glass

the avatar and plus button on Today, Spark's various buttons and input bar, the camera's flash and zoom controls — all got the glass treatment, so they look more at home on the new system.
